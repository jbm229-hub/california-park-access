Name: Joshua Mendez
Course: 595 – Command-Line GIS

Project Overview

This project examines how accessible California’s state and national parks are using both automobile and pedestrian travel metrics. Accessibility is measured using a traffic-weighted drivability score and walking-time isochrones derived from OpenStreetMap pedestrian networks. Demographic characteristics of populations within walking distance of parks are summarized using U.S. Census American Community Survey (ACS) tract-level data.

Data Sources and Preparation

Transportation and Park Data

  • California Annual Average Daily Traffic (AADT):
    Obtained from gis.data.ca.gov, prepared by the California Department of Transportation (Caltrans). Data were originally provided as line and point geometries in GeoJSON format and represent recent traffic counts on major roadways.
  • California State Park Boundaries:
    Downloaded from parks.ca.gov, originally provided as a shapefile. Boundaries were reprojected and clipped to the California state boundary.
  • National Park Service Points of Interest:
    Provided by the National Park Service as a GeoJSON dataset. Only visitor centers were retained to represent meaningful park access points.

Census Data

  • U.S. Census Tracts (2023):
    Downloaded from the U.S. Census TIGER/Line files.
  • American Community Survey (ACS) 5-Year Estimates:
    Population, median age, and median household income were accessed via the Census API at the tract level.

Data Processing and Quality Considerations

  • All datasets were projected to a common CRS (EPSG:4326) for spatial analysis.
  • Spatial joins were used to associate census tracts with metropolitan areas and to intersect tracts with walking isochrones.
  • Traffic counts were converted to numeric values and missing or invalid AADT values were set to zero.
  • Demographic summaries within isochrones were area-weighted to account for partial tract overlaps.
